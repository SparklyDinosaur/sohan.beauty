💌 Love Letters

Letters to the world, to the community, to love itself. Written by Sohan — proudly, openly, unapologetically gay.

"To love and be loved is to feel the sun from both sides." — David Viscott

🌈
To: Every Gay Kid Who Felt Alone
April 2026

You Were Never Broken

I know what it's like to lie awake at night feeling like there's something fundamentally wrong with you. Like you're a glitch. Like you got the wrong settings installed at birth. I need you to know — and I need you to hear this clearly — there is nothing wrong with you. There never was.

The world got it wrong. Not you. The thing they told you was a flaw is actually a gift. The love you're capable of, the way you see beauty in unexpected places, the depth of feeling that comes with being different — that is not a burden. That is your power.

Somewhere out there is a version of your life that is so full of joy it would make your current self weep. A chosen family who loves you fiercely. A community who gets it. People who will celebrate every single version of you. I promise. You just have to stay long enough to find them.

With all my love, Sohan 🌈
💜 Community 🏳️‍🌈 Pride ✨ Hope

To: A Future Lover
April 2026

I Don't Know You Yet But I Already Know You're Worth It

I haven't met you yet. But I've thought about you — the way you probably laugh too loud at your own jokes, the playlist you have on at 2am, the way your eyes look when you talk about something you really love.

I want to know all of it. Every embarrassing story. Every song that makes you cry. The thing you're afraid to tell people on a first date but tell me anyway by the third. I want to know what you looked like at sixteen when you were figuring yourself out, and who you've become since, and who you're still becoming.

I'm not in a rush. Love at its best doesn't rush. But when I find you, I'm going to love you so loudly and so well that you'll wonder how you ever doubted you deserved it. Because you do. Unequivocally. Completely. With rainbow confetti.

Saving you a seat, Sohan 💜
💖 Romance 🌹 Love 💫 Future

To: The Straight People Who Supported Me
March 2026

Thank You For Showing Up Without Being Asked

You know who you are. The friends who never made it weird. Who put the pride flag on their Instagram story in June and kept it in their heart all year. Who shut down the homophobic joke at the table before I even had to decide whether to react.

I know it cost you something — the comfort of staying quiet, the ease of going along. I know it wasn't always convenient. I know you didn't do it for credit. That's exactly why I want to give you some.

You made more difference than you know. Every time someone like you chose the harder, kinder path, you made the world I live in slightly safer. You reminded me that love crosses every line people try to draw. You're part of my story, and I'm so glad you are.

Genuinely grateful, Sohan 🏳️‍🌈
🤍 Allyship 💙 Gratitude 🏳️‍🌈 Pride

To: Myself, Age 15
February 2026

Stop Trying So Hard to Fit Inside a Box That Was Never Built for You

I know you're exhausted. Performing straightness is the most tiring thing I have ever done, and I did it for years. The laughing at jokes that made me feel sick. The deliberate looking the wrong way. The calculated amount of interest in girls.

Here's what I wish I could tell you: the box was always too small. It was small for everyone who ever tried to fit into it. And outside the box — out here where I am now — it's warmer than you think. There are people here who will love you for exactly what you are. Not despite it. Because of it.

The coming out you're so afraid of? It's going to feel like the first full breath you've taken in years. You are going to be so okay. More than okay. You are going to be Sohan. Fully. Loudly. Finally. And it's going to be beautiful.

Love you more than you know, Sohan ✨
💫 Self-love 🌈 Coming Out 💪 Growth

To: The UC9 Group Chat
April 2026

You Are My Chosen Family and I Will Embarrass You With How Much I Love You

I'm writing this at an hour when most people are asleep, and the group chat has gone quiet for once, and I just want to say: I love you all so much it's genuinely unhinged. Like clinically. Someone should study it.

You are the people who showed up. Not just when it was easy — when it wasn't. When I needed someone to sit with me in the hard parts. When I needed to be silly without explanation. When I needed to be celebrated for absolutely nothing at all. You were there for all of it.

UC9 isn't a group. It's a feeling. It's the specific feeling of being known and loved at the same time, which is the rarest thing in the world and I don't take it for granted for a single second. Thank you. Embarrassingly, overwhelmingly, thank you.

Forever yours, Sohan 💖 (please don't screenshot this)
